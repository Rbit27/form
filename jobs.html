§ 01 · Why FORM

The mission doesn't
exploit users.

We built the clinical-safety layer first — not as an afterthought or compliance checkbox. No shame, no before/after framing, no metrics that make users feel bad. Health technology that respects the person using it.

The craft bar is
unusually high.

Every HTML prototype, every doc, every line of copy is reviewed for AI-slop aesthetics. We have a brand-system agent that enforces token consistency. A brand-voice agent that catches anything Victor wouldn't actually say. If you care about craft, you'll care about working here.

The equity is
real founding equity.

Founding Engineer: 1.5–3.0%. Design Lead: 1.0–2.0%. 4-year vest, 1-year cliff, standard terms. You're not "early employee #47." You're the first two humans on this team. That means the equity reflects it.

The timing means
you shape it.

Pre-product means you choose the architecture. You make the decisions that compound for years. The roadmap exists, the strategy is documented, the platform is planned — but the execution is yours to define. If you want to inherit a system, this isn't the role. If you want to build one, it is.

§ 03 · What We Value
01

Craft over
output velocity.

Shipping fast and shipping well are different things. We don't measure PRs per week. We measure whether the thing we shipped is actually good. Speed will come when foundations are right.

02

Safety is the
product.

clinical-safety has a hard veto on everything we ship. Not a review step — a block. This changes how we build. The guardrails are the brand, not the limitation.

03

Use what you
build.

If you don't train, don't apply. Not because we require athletes — because building a product you'd personally use produces fundamentally better decisions than building for an imagined user.

04

Direct, not
diplomatic.

We expect disagreement. We expect it to be voiced clearly, not softened into ambiguity. Victor doesn't bullshit users. We don't bullshit each other.

05

Remote-first, not
remote-only.

Async is default. Quarterly offsites — Kyiv, Lviv, or Berlin — are mandatory. Deep work needs space, but trust needs presence. We optimize for both.

06

No fabricated
numbers.

Goals are OK. Results only when data exists. This applies to our investor materials, our content, our product claims. If we don't have the data yet, we say so. We built-in-public for a reason.

§ 04 · Interview Process

We aim for six steps over 2–3 weeks. No surprise rounds. No trick questions. We want to see your real work and judgment — not whether you can perform under artificial pressure.

01
Portfolio or work review (async)

Send us work, not a CV. For engineers: a GitHub project, an App Store link, or a production codebase section you're proud of. For designers: a Figma file or shipped iOS screen with annotation of decisions you made. We review and respond within 3 business days.

02
30-min intro call (motivation + fit)

What interests you about this problem specifically? What did you understand about FORM before this call? What would you want to build here that you couldn't build elsewhere? We're not checking enthusiasm — we're checking specificity.

03
Take-home (8 hours maximum)

For engineers: a minimal pose-detection prototype — use AVFoundation to capture video, detect a single body joint using Vision framework, log rep counts. Show your architecture decisions, not just output.

For designers: redesign one section of our index.html in Figma. Show the before, the decision rationale, the token structure you'd apply. Keep the editorial-brutalist aesthetic — or argue why it should change.

04
90-min deep technical / craft session

Walk us through your take-home solution. Then we'll go deep on decisions: why this approach, what you'd do differently, how you'd scale it. For engineers, we may pair on a live extension of the prototype. For designers, we'll push on edge cases and dark patterns you might have missed.

05
60-min founder conversation

George. No agenda beyond: how do you think? What would you change about what we've built? What would you build in the first 30 days? We want to understand your judgment, your taste, and whether you'd actually want to work here after seeing behind the curtain.

06
Reference checks + offer

Three references: direct manager, peer, and if applicable a direct report. We'll ask about frustrations — not just strengths. If everything checks out, offer within 48 hours. Equity terms are transparent before the call, not a surprise at the end.

Timeline: we aim to complete from first contact to offer in 2–3 weeks. We'll tell you where you stand after every step. No ghosting.
