# Why we're building FORM

**Subject:** Why we're building FORM
**Format:** Personal letter
**Target:** 400–600 words
**Audience:** Self-coached lifters on the early-access list
**Tone:** Victor off-duty — direct, honest, no performance
**Status:** DRAFT — pending clinical-safety review before send

---

First year of lifting, I trained with a coach.

Two sessions a week. In-person. He watched every rep.

Not because my form was terrible — it wasn't. Something else happens when another person who knows what they're doing is in the room. They see the half-rep on set three. They notice the hip shift that shows up when you're tired. They say "stop" before you chase a number into an injury.

Then the sessions ended. Budget. Schedule. Life.

I downloaded the apps.

All of them, eventually. Heart rate trackers. Rep counters. AI suggestion engines that pulled from a generic database with no idea what I'd done yesterday, or whether I'd slept five hours, or that my left shoulder was being careful since February.

They weren't coaches. They were logbooks with better fonts.

The gap isn't a features gap. It's a category gap.

Every fitness app is trying to be a better tracker. Nobody's trying to be a trainer. The difference is: a tracker records what happened. A trainer sees what's happening, makes a call, and adjusts.

FORM is the attempt to build the trainer.

That means three things in practice:

Camera-based form feedback on eight base lifts — squat, deadlift, bench, overhead press, row, pull-up, hip hinge, lunge. Real-time. From the camera already in your pocket.

A program that reads your HRV and sleep data each morning and adjusts load accordingly. Not because you hit a target, but because your body's readiness changes day to day and a good coach responds to that.

A coaching voice that says three words where other apps write a paragraph. We call it Victor. He doesn't do motivational posters.

That's it. No transformation promises. No before/after. No lifetime deals.

---

If you take training seriously and you're tired of apps that don't — you're in the right place.

First 200 beta slots go to people on this list. When they open, you get the email first.
