### Post L-01 — "Why I'm building FORM"
**Type:** Founder story · EN · Use early in sequence

---

I've had two types of coaches in my life.

The first kind corrected my form, knew when to push and when to back off, noticed when I was sleeping badly before I said a word. That coach cost €120/hour and I could afford him three times.

The second kind was an app. It told me to do 3×8 squats regardless of whether I'd slept 5 hours or 8. It didn't know my back was tight. It didn't care.

The gap between those two experiences is what I'm building FORM to close.

FORM is an AI trainer that watches your movement through your phone camera, reads your HRV and sleep from your wearable, and adapts what you're doing — in real time — like that first coach would.

No free tier. No ads. No gamification. Just a trainer that actually pays attention.

We're pre-launch. Building in public from Kyiv.

If you've felt that gap — I'd love to hear about your experience. 15 minutes. No pitch. Just research.

### Post L-04 — "The form problem"
**Type:** Insight-driven · EN · High-reach potential

---

Every gym has the same person.

They've been training for 3 years. Their squat looks almost right. The bar path drifts forward on rep 6 of 8. They've been told "chest up" a hundred times. It doesn't stick.

Not because they're not trying. Because verbal cues without real-time visual feedback don't transfer.

A good coach would: watch the movement, identify the compensation pattern, give a cue at the exact moment of error, and check if it worked next rep.

Apps give you: a video to watch later. Or nothing.

FORM watches every rep, gives the cue at the moment of error, in Victor's voice.

The technology exists. We're applying it to the one place it hasn't been applied seriously yet.

### Post L-05 — "Why HRV matters for training"
**Type:** Thought leadership · EN · Educational

---

HRV is not a performance metric.

It's a readiness metric. There's a difference.

High HRV doesn't mean you'll lift more today. It means your nervous system has recovered and can absorb training stimulus. Low HRV doesn't mean rest — it means the stimulus needs to change.

Most fitness apps ignore this or turn it into a binary (green/yellow/red). That's not coaching. That's a traffic light.

FORM reads your HRV trend (not just today's number), cross-references with sleep quality and recent training load, and adjusts volume and intensity accordingly.

A 3% intensity reduction on a low-readiness day prevents the overtraining that costs you 10 days of regression.

Small adjustments. Compounding over months.

### Post L-06 — "Why no free tier"
**Type:** Controversial take · EN · Designed for debate

---

We're building a premium AI trainer. We won't have a free tier. Ever.

Here's why this is better for you as a user:

Free tiers require monetizing your data or your attention. Neither is compatible with a health app that knows your body composition, sleep patterns, and movement.

Free tiers create two-class experiences. Features go behind paywalls. The free product becomes a funnel, not a product.

Free tiers attract users who won't convert. Then the company optimizes for the wrong cohort.

FORM: $9/month in UA, $19/month in EU.

That's the price of two coffees. For a coach that trains with you every day.

If the value isn't worth $9/month, we haven't built what we're trying to build.

### Post L-10 — "What it's like to be first"
**Type:** Hiring · EN · Use 2-3 weeks after first JD posts

---

A founding hire is different from a hire.

You're not joining a process. You're building the process.

You're not inheriting architecture. You're making architectural decisions that will compound for 5 years.

You're not implementing features. You're deciding which features exist.

The upside: equity, speed, ownership, direct impact. Your name on the thing.

The downside: ambiguity is the job. There's no product spec waiting for you. There's a direction and a set of non-negotiables.

For the Founding Engineer: the non-negotiables are real-time CV feedback quality, wearable integration depth, and privacy-first data model. Everything else is negotiable.

If that sounds like freedom rather than anxiety — we should talk.
