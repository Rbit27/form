# Cycle-Aware Training · Sample Week

> **Disclaimer:** Доказова база для cycle-syncing тренувань — модератна, не максимальна. Ми позиціонуємо це як **рамку**, не як магічну оптимізацію. Реальна користь — у самосвідомості, не у точних формулах.
>
> Для жінок без регулярного циклу (TRT, hormonal birth control, menopause, тощо) — цей шаблон не застосовується. Регулярна програма.

---

## Контекст

**Цикл фази:**

| Phase | Дні | Hormones | Training response (typical) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Menstrual** | 1-5 | Low E2, low P4 | Energy varies; some feel strong, some tired |
| **Follicular** | 6-14 | Rising E2 | Generally high energy, strength up |
| **Ovulation** | 13-15 (~day 14) | E2 peak | Peak strength для many |
| **Luteal early** | 15-22 | P4 rises | Good performance, mood варіюється |
| **Luteal late** | 23-28 | Both decline | Lower energy, higher RPE for same load |

**Caveats:**
- Individual variation huge — your patterns можуть відрізнятися
- Track 2-3 цикли минулим, побачиш свій pattern
- Не для всіх strength varies — деякі fitness data NOT moves through cycle

---

## Підхід: «рамка», не «правила»

**Three foundation principles:**

1. **Train hard коли почуваєшся сильною** — не уникай challenge
2. **Adapt intensity коли почуваєшся менш потужно** — не ламай себе
3. **Track симптоми** — будуй власні patterns over months

## Sample week — variable by phase

### Phase: Follicular (typical day 8-13)

**Profile:** Rising estrogen, peak energy for many.

```
Mon · Lower A — heavy squat focus
Tue · Upper A — push
Wed · Rest або mobility
Thu · Lower B — heavy deadlift focus
Fri · Upper B — pull
Sat · Optional accessory
Sun · Rest
```

**Key calibration:**
- Heavy compound lifts up to RPE 8-9 — push it
- High volume tolerable
- 1RM testing window (day 11-13)
- Carbs can be slightly higher (energy demands)

---

### Phase: Ovulation (typical day 14)

**Profile:** Estrogen peak, often peak strength.

```
Mon · Lower A — try max efforts
Tue · Upper A — push, possible PR
Wed · Rest
Thu · Lower B — heavy
Fri · Upper B
```

**Key calibration:**
- 1RM attempts realistic here
- Joint mobility may feel «loose» (estrogen affects ligaments — be mindful of form)
- Risk of injury slightly elevated через ligamentous laxity — focus on technique

---

### Phase: Luteal Early (typical day 15-22)

**Profile:** Progesterone rising, generally still good performance.

```
Mon · Lower A — moderate-heavy
Tue · Upper A
Wed · Rest
Thu · Lower B — moderate-heavy
Fri · Upper B
Sat · Optional accessory
```

**Key calibration:**
- Performance generally still good
- Body temperature slightly elevated (cardio може feel harder)
- Hydration extra important
- Sleep prioritize

---

### Phase: Luteal Late (typical day 23-28)

**Profile:** Both hormones declining, often lower energy, PMS symptoms possible.

```
Mon · Lower A — light-moderate (RPE 7)
Tue · Upper A — focus reps over weight
Wed · Rest
Thu · Optional — listen to body
Fri · Mobility focus
```

**Key calibration:**
- Reduce intensity на 5-10% від follicular peaks
- Reduce volume by 1-2 sets per exercise
- Higher reps, lower weight (RPE-target stays similar; weight changes)
- Hydration, sleep extra critical
- Cramps / mood / bloating — normal, не failure

---

### Phase: Menstrual (typical day 1-5)

**Profile:** Hormones at low. Many feel relief from luteal-late symptoms.

```
Day 1-2 · Listen to body. Often лушче rest або very light movement.
Day 3-5 · Most women report return of energy. Resume normal training.
```

**Key calibration:**
- First 1-2 days: optional rest or gentle yoga / walk
- Don't force heavy training day 1-2
- Track strength — many report bounce-back by day 3
- Iron-rich foods important post-period

## What changes vs men's program

### Changes (cycle-aware adds)

1. **Weekly volume varies ±15-20%** by phase
2. **Heavy lifts gated by phase** — try heaviest in follicular/ovulation
3. **Rest days flex by symptoms** — luteal late може deserve extra rest
4. **Nutrition timing adapts:**
   - Iron-focus у menstrual phase
   - Carbs slightly higher у follicular (high energy demand)
   - Sodium / fluid balance attention у luteal

### What stays same

1. **Total weekly volume per muscle group** — bodybuilding science doesn't change by cycle
2. **Programming structure** — periodization works same way
3. **Recovery science** — sleep, HRV, protein — universal
4. **Safety floor** — same standards

## Nutrition adjustments

### Per phase

| Phase | Calorie target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Menstrual | Same or +50 | Iron-rich foods (lean red meat, spinach, lentils) |
| Follicular | Same | Carbs slightly higher OK if hungry |
| Ovulation | Same | Hydration high |
| Luteal early | Same | Normal eating |
| Luteal late | +100-200 typical | Many women genuinely hungrier — okay to eat |

**Anti-pattern:** Treating luteal-late hunger as «willpower failure». It's not. Eat the food. Real biological signal.

## Edge cases

### Hormonal birth control

- Many oral contraceptives suppress natural cycle variations
- Cycle-aware features less relevant
- App detects via Apple Health if marked — switches to «regular» program

### Irregular cycles

- If cycles vary > 14 days month-to-month — track but don't auto-adjust
- Recommend consultation with health professional
- App can still help з general programming

### Menopause / perimenopause

- Cycle-syncing less applicable
- Focus shifts to:
  - Strength preservation (bone density)
  - Cardiovascular health
  - Sleep quality (common disruption period)
  - Joint mobility

### Athletes у training blocks

- Some Olympic / professional athletes purposefully suppress cycle (medical decision)
- We don't recommend this — but accept user's medical context
- Adapt programming to their actual physiology

## Open scientific questions

1. **How much does strength actually vary by phase?**
   - Older studies (2000s): claimed significant variation
   - Newer meta-analyses (2020+): variation smaller, individually variable
   - We position humbly

2. **Does ligamentous laxity у ovulation increase injury risk?**
   - Studies inconsistent
   - We mention as note, not gospel
   - Focus on form quality always > timing-magic

3. **Is luteal-phase strength loss real or perceptual?**
   - Some say it's primarily mood/perception, не physiology
   - We honor user's lived experience
