## 1 · Why LinkedIn is different from X

| Dimension | X / Twitter | LinkedIn |
|---|---|---|
| Primary audience | Fitness enthusiasts, tech Twitter, investors who post publicly | Hiring candidates, investors doing research, fitness industry professionals |
| Algorithm | Engagement velocity, reply threads, quote-tweets | Dwell time, comments, personal connection graph |
| Content length | 280 chars (posts) / threads | 300 chars (above-fold hook) / 1300 chars optimal body |
| Tone | Direct, punchy, controversial OK | Honest, professional, still direct — no corporate-speak |
| Cadence | 5-7 posts/week | 2-3 posts/week |
| Connection model | Follower-based (asymmetric) | Connection-based (symmetric) — relationship graph matters |
| Value for FORM | Build in public, beta recruiting, community | Hiring signal, investor visibility, industry credibility |
| Key metric | Impressions, engagement rate | Profile views, connection requests, InMail replies |

**The core insight:** LinkedIn reaches people who are not on Fitness Twitter. Investors who won't see your X thread will read your LinkedIn post. Engineers who aren't following build-in-public accounts will click through from a LinkedIn job post. Fitness industry professionals who need to be interviewed check LinkedIn daily.

Use both platforms. Do not substitute one for the other.

## 2 · Profile setup (George's personal profile)

Do not create a company page yet. In the pre-product phase, people invest in founders, not companies. Your personal profile IS the brand presence.

### Headline

LinkedIn shows ~220 characters of headline in search results. The default is your current job title — override it.

**Recommended headline:**
```
Founder @ FORM · Building an AI coach for serious lifters · Camera form analysis + HRV-aware programming · Kyiv
```

Why this works: "AI coach for serious lifters" filters your audience correctly (not general wellness, not gamified fitness, serious training). "Camera form analysis + HRV-aware" tells technically curious people what the hard problem is. "Kyiv" signals Ukrainian tech ecosystem, which carries signal for the right investors.

**Alternative — more minimal:**
```
Building FORM — AI personal trainer · No free tier, no ads, no BS · iOS · pre-launch
```

Test both. The first is better for search (specific keywords). The second is better for curiosity-click.

### About section

Write this in first person, direct, no third person, no "I am a passionate entrepreneur." Two paragraphs max. 

Draft:

```
I've had two types of coaches in my life. The first one cost €120/hour — he corrected my form, 
knew when to push and when to back off, noticed when I was sleeping badly before I said a word. 
The second was an app. It didn't care.

FORM closes that gap. AI trainer that watches your movement through your phone camera, reads your 
HRV and sleep, and adapts what you do — in real time — like that first coach would. 
Pre-launch. Building in public. github.com/Rbit27/form
```

Keep it to ~150 words. The goal is not to explain everything — it is to make the right people curious and give them a link.

### Featured section

Pin two items max:
1. Link to `github.com/Rbit27/form` — "We're building in public. Everything is here."
2. When beta.html is live: link to the beta application page.

Do not pin LinkedIn posts you wrote. Pin destinations that capture intent (GitHub shows seriousness, beta form captures leads).

### Experience section

List "Founder, FORM" with the start date. Description: 3 bullets, plain language.

### Creator Mode

- "Follow" button as primary CTA (vs "Connect" — better for building audience pre-relationship)

Hashtags to feature: `#buildinpublic` `#fitnesstech` `#startupfounder`

## 3 · Company page

**Recommendation: do not create a company page before beta.**

Reasons:
- A company page with 0 followers and 0 posts signals nothing. It weakens credibility.
- LinkedIn company pages require separate content management.
- Personal profile has 10x better organic reach.
- Investors and candidates look at the founder, not the company page.

Create company page when:
- App is live OR
- Beta has 100+ users OR
- You need it for sponsored content (not pre-launch)

Until then, all LinkedIn presence = George's personal profile.

## 4 · Content strategy

The `content/linkedin-posts.md` file has 12 posts ready. This section covers how to use them.

### Four pillars (LinkedIn-specific)

LinkedIn rewards posts that have a clear perspective, a specific audience, and a credibility signal. The four pillars for FORM:

**Pillar 1 — Founder story**
Who reads it: investors, potential hires, journalists, people considering signing up for beta.
Format: personal narrative, specific moment, broader lesson. 
Posts available: L-01, L-02, L-03.

**Pillar 2 — Product insight / thought leadership**
Who reads it: fitness industry professionals, investors validating thesis, potential beta users.
Format: specific claim, evidence or reasoning, FORM's position.
Posts available: L-04, L-05, L-06.

**Pillar 3 — Hiring signal**
Who reads it: candidates, their networks, people who might refer candidates.
Format: what we're building + what we need + what we offer. Direct. No "exciting opportunity."
Posts available: L-08, L-09, L-10.

**Pillar 4 — Build-in-public**
Who reads it: everyone. Most shareable. Builds credibility with all audiences simultaneously.
Format: specific deliverable + honest reflection + link to evidence.
Posts available: L-07, L-11, L-12.

### Sequence logic

Start with Pillar 1 (story), then alternate Pillar 2 and Pillar 4, insert Pillar 3 when JDs go live.

Do not start with a hiring post. You need to establish who you are before people will believe the role is worth applying for.

See `content/linkedin-posts.md` → Posting Calendar for week-by-week sequence.

### Writing new posts (beyond the 12)

When you need new content, use this structure:

```
Line 1: Hook. Specific, surprising, or counterintuitive statement. Not "I'm excited to share."
[blank line]
Body: 3-5 short paragraphs OR a numbered list. Max 1,300 characters total.
[blank line]
Close: What FORM does differently, or a genuine question to invite comments.
[blank line]
Optional link in first comment (not in the post body — algorithm penalizes external links in posts).
```

Example hook quality check:
- Bad: "Today I want to talk about why fitness apps fail."
- Good: "Every fitness app has the same problem. It collects data. Then nothing happens."

The hook is the only thing most people will see before deciding to expand. It must earn the click.

## 5 · Connection strategy

LinkedIn is a connection graph. Who you're connected to determines who sees your posts organically. Build the connection network intentionally.

### Priority tiers

**Tier 1 — Connect immediately (target: 50 connections in week 1)**

These people have high probability of engaging with your content and amplifying it:

- Ukrainian tech founders building B2C products (Grammarly, MacPaw, Preply, etc.)
- iOS/Swift engineers in Ukraine and Eastern Europe (active candidates AND potential referrers)
- Health/fitness tech founders globally (people doing adjacent things — follow, don't compete)
- Sports scientists and fitness coaches with online presence
- Pre-seed/seed investors with public interest in health or fitness tech

How to find them: search LinkedIn for "[role] Ukraine", "[role] Kyiv", "health tech founder", "fitness app founder." Filter by 2nd-degree connections first.

Connection note (always send a personalized note — it triples acceptance rate):
```
Hi [name], building FORM — AI personal trainer with CV form analysis from Kyiv. 
Followed your work on [specific thing]. Would be glad to connect.
```

Do not send a pitch in the connection note. Just signal relevance.

**Tier 2 — Connect before outreach (target: 20 connections, ongoing)**

- Potential investors you plan to pitch (connect 2-3 weeks before cold outreach — warm connection matters)
- Journalists covering health tech or Ukrainian startups
- Fitness industry executives at relevant companies (wearable brands, gym chains)
- Sport science academics (validation, advisory board potential)

**Tier 3 — Follow, don't connect (ongoing)**

- Major tech/fitness accounts where connection would feel presumptuous
- Company pages (Whoop, Oura, Garmin, Future, Apple Fitness+)
- Relevant hashtag pages

### Engagement before connection

Before sending a connection request to a high-value target (investor, senior hire, key journalist), comment meaningfully on 2-3 of their posts first. Then connect. Your connection request will have context.

"Meaningful comment" = adds a specific perspective, asks a genuine question, or extends the argument. Not "Great post!" That signals you're farming connections.

## 6 · LinkedIn for hiring

Hiring is where LinkedIn provides direct, measurable ROI. Here is how to run it.

### When JDs go live

1. Publish Post L-08 (Founding Engineer) and Post L-09 (Design Lead) — see `content/linkedin-posts.md`
2. In the same week, share the GitHub JD link (`hiring/jd-founding-engineer-ios.md`) in a follow-up comment on each post
3. Ask 3-5 connections to share or comment — warm signal extends organic reach dramatically
4. In the first 60 minutes after posting, reply to every comment — algorithm rewards early engagement velocity

### InMail vs connection + note

For senior engineering candidates: connection request + note > InMail. InMails feel cold from unknown senders. A note with a connection request has context.

Template:
```
Hi [name], I'm building FORM — AI personal trainer with computer vision form analysis, iOS-first. 
Looking for a Founding Engineer to own the entire iOS stack. Your work on [specific project] 
is exactly the kind of thing we need. Full role: github.com/Rbit27/form/hiring/jd-founding-engineer-ios.md

Happy to share more if relevant.
```

Keep it under 150 words. Not a pitch deck, not a job description — a signal.

### Passive reach: posting about the role regularly

One post per role is not enough. LinkedIn organic reach is narrow. Plan:

- Week 1: Hiring post (L-08 or L-09)
- Week 4: L-10 "What it's like to be first" — frames the opportunity
- Week 8: Update post — "Still open, here's what we learned from interviews so far"
- When role is filled: announce who joined and why they chose FORM (strong trust signal)

### Platform integration

LinkedIn is ONE channel for hiring. Run it in parallel with:
- Djinni (primary for Ukrainian iOS engineers)
- DOU.ua jobs board
- Wellfound (for international reach)

Full platform-by-platform posting guide: `docs/HIRING_GUIDE.md`

## 7 · LinkedIn for investor relationships

Investors research founders before taking meetings. Your LinkedIn profile will be checked before every intro call. Make sure it answers the question "does this person know what they're doing?"

### What investors read on LinkedIn

1. Your headline and recent activity (last 3-4 posts)
2. Your About section
3. Mutual connections — who you both know
4. Content quality and consistency (are you thinking seriously about the problem?)

### Visibility strategy

Post thought-leadership content (Pillar 2) that investors would share with their networks. Topics that perform well with VC audiences:

- Non-obvious market observations ("Why fitness is a broken category for AI")
- Founder decision transparency ("We decided not to have a free tier — here's the math")
- Real technical challenges ("CV form analysis is 10x harder than we expected for X reason")

This is not about hacking the algorithm. It is about being worth following before a meeting.

### LinkedIn and warm intros

When requesting a warm intro through LinkedIn:
1. Find the mutual connection
2. Check if they're 1st-degree connected to both you and the target investor
3. Send the connection request note first (2 weeks before asking for intro)
4. Ask for intro via private message, not InMail

Intro request template (from `docs/INVESTOR_PIPELINE.md` §5 — Warm Intro Request):
Same approach applies via LinkedIn message. Do not use InMail for intro requests — it signals you don't have the connection.

### Who to connect with in VC

Target: Associates and Analysts at relevant funds (they do the initial research, they see everything, they can champion internally). Partners at smaller funds. Operators-turned-investors in health/fitness.

Do not waste time trying to connect with Tier 1 VC General Partners via LinkedIn cold. Those relationships need warmer paths — mutual founders, advisors, events.

## 8 · Build-in-public on LinkedIn

LinkedIn is better for narrative build-in-public than X. Here is why: longer format lets you explain the "why" behind a decision, not just announce it. An investor or engineer reading LinkedIn will spend 2-3 minutes on your post. X: 15 seconds.

### What translates from X

- Product decisions with honest reasoning
- Weekly progress updates (L-11 template)
- Honest failures ("we decided against X and here's why")
- Team/hiring milestones

### What does NOT translate

- Twitter threads (copy-paste of a thread reads terribly on LinkedIn — rewrite as a single post)
- Real-time commentary / reactions to news
- Very short punchy posts (they feel thin on LinkedIn)
- Meme-style content

### Repurposing blog content

Each blog post draft in `content/` can generate one LinkedIn post. Format:

- Hook from the first paragraph of the blog post (modified for LinkedIn length)
- 3-5 key points from the post
- Link to full post in first comment (not in the body)
- Ask a specific question at the end to drive comments

This is not copy-paste. It is extraction. Read the blog, find the sharpest insight, build a standalone LinkedIn post around it. The blog link is proof, not the lead.

## 9 · Cadence

LinkedIn punishes over-posting. More than 3 posts per week on a personal profile starts to feel like spam in the feed. 

**Recommended cadence: 2-3 posts per week**

| Day | Content type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tuesday | Pillar 2 or 4 (insight or build-in-public) | Tuesday AM has best reach |
| Thursday | Pillar 1 or 3 (story or hiring) | Thursday PM also good |
| (Optional) Saturday | Engagement post — question, observation | Shorter, lower stakes |

Do not post Monday morning (everyone is overwhelmed). Do not post Friday afternoon (no one is engaging).

**First 60 minutes rule:** After posting, reply to every comment within 60 minutes. LinkedIn algorithm scores early engagement velocity. One engaged discussion in the first hour matters more than 10 comments 8 hours later.

**Re-sharing rhythm:** If a post performs well (100+ reactions), don't re-share it. Write a follow-up post that goes deeper on one point. This shows intellectual momentum, not recycling.

## 10 · Voice rules

LinkedIn is not X. It is not your newsletter. Different format, different register.

| Rule | LinkedIn | X / Twitter |
|---|---|---|
| Opening line | Specific claim or story beat — earns the expand | Can be punchy fragment |
| Length | 300-1300 characters optimal. Shorter feels lazy. Longer loses people. | 280 or threads |
| Tone | Direct and professional. Honest but polished. | Raw, faster, more reactive OK |
| Humor | Wry observation OK. Self-deprecating OK. Never try-hard jokes. | Same, but more tolerance for irreverence |
| Hashtags | 3-5, in first comment or bottom of post | 2-3, in body OK |
| External links | First comment (not post body — penalized) | In body fine |
| Emojis | Use sparingly. One bullet-point marker or one emphasis. Not decorative. | More flexible |
| Personal | Yes. LinkedIn rewards genuine human voice. | Yes, same. |
| Victor's voice | Never. LinkedIn = George's voice. Victor is the AI trainer, not the founder. | Same. |

**The single most important rule:** never write "I'm excited to share." Never write "I'm humbled." Never write "game-changer." These phrases signal inauthenticity and kill engagement. Write how you would speak to a smart colleague over coffee.

## 11 · Metrics

LinkedIn provides native analytics for posts and the profile. Track these:

### Post-level

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Impressions | n/a (varies wildly by algorithm) | Track trend, not absolute |
| Engagement rate | > 2% (reactions + comments / impressions) | < 1% means hook needs work |
| Comments | Quality over quantity | 5 substantive comments > 50 reactions |
| Profile views after post | +30-50% spike | Signal of content driving discovery |

### Profile-level

| Metric | What it means |
|---|---|
| Profile views (weekly) | Awareness — are the right people finding you? |
| Search appearances | Are your keywords working? (Shows what searches led here) |
| Connection request rate | After posting, are the right people following up? |
| InMail opens | Are cold pitches to engineers and investors getting read? |

### LinkedIn Social Selling Index (SSI)

LinkedIn provides an SSI score (0-100) visible at `linkedin.com/sales/ssi`. Track it weekly. Components:
- **Establish professional brand** (profile completeness, engagement with your content)
- **Find the right people** (search quality, InMail use)
- **Engage with insights** (comment quality, shares)
- **Build relationships** (connection growth, connection quality)

Target SSI > 60 within 4 weeks of active use. It is a proxy metric, not a goal — do not optimize for SSI directly.

### Hiring metrics (when JDs are live)

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Post reach on hiring posts | > 5,000 impressions |
| InMail reply rate | > 25% (if < 15%, rewrite the message) |
| Applications via LinkedIn referral | Track UTM — `?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=post` |
| Quality applications | This is the only metric that matters — define "quality" by rubric in `docs/HIRING.md` |

## 12 · Honest limitations

Do not expect LinkedIn to do things it cannot do.

**LinkedIn will NOT:**
- Drive significant App Store installs (LinkedIn audience skews professional, not daily-fitness-tracker)
- Build a large consumer audience before launch (consumers are not on LinkedIn for product discovery)
- Replace X for real-time community and beta recruiting
- Produce viral growth for a fitness consumer app

**LinkedIn WILL:**
- Produce qualified engineering and design candidates
- Build credibility with investors before your first meeting
- Give fitness industry professionals and journalists a signal that you're serious
- Allow you to research targets (investors, potential hires, advisors) and warm connections before outreach
- Generate occasional surprise conversations with people who matter

**The honest use case for FORM on LinkedIn:** it is a credibility and hiring channel, not a growth channel. Treat it accordingly. Do not measure success by followers. Measure it by quality conversations started.

## 15 · Open questions

1. **George's current LinkedIn network size** — if it's < 200, prioritize Tier 1 connection growth before the first post goes out. Organic reach scales with connection count.

2. **Language split** — `content/linkedin-posts.md` has posts in EN and some in UA. LinkedIn allows one language per post. Decision: use EN as default (broader reach), UA for Ukraine-specific stories (L-02). This is a `brand-voice` decision — revisit if user research shows the beta audience is primarily Ukrainian.

3. **LinkedIn newsletter vs Beehiiv** — LinkedIn has a native newsletter feature (under Creator Mode). It is tempting because it reaches connections directly. However: it locks your audience in LinkedIn. Beehiiv gives you the email list. Recommendation: use Beehiiv as primary newsletter (`docs/NEWSLETTER.md`). Use LinkedIn newsletter only for a quarterly update that cross-promotes Beehiiv. Revisit if Beehiiv setup is blocked.

4. **George's export/import of existing network** — If George has an existing LinkedIn network from previous professional life, segment it before posting. Fitness tech build-in-public content may not land with all audiences equally. LinkedIn's "post to connections" vs "anyone" toggle manages this partially.
