# FORM · Investor Update Template v0.1

> Monthly update template. Same structure each time. Easier to read, easier to write.
> Власник: founder. Send by 14-th day of each month for previous.

---

## Cadence rules

1. **Monthly, без exceptions.** Including bad months. Especially bad months.
2. **Send by day 14** of next month
3. **Length: under 600 words** ideally
4. **Replyable** — include direct ask или question
5. **Honest numbers** — never spin

## Detailed section guidance

### Subject line
- Format: `FORM · [Month Year] update (M[n])`
- M-number = months since founding (M1 = first month)
- Easy to filter, easy to search

### Opening
- One sentence
- No «hope you're well» pleasantries

### Key metrics
- **Same metrics every month** (так easier to compare)
- Include trend arrow (↑↓→)
- Include % change from previous
- Don't hide bad numbers — investors want truth

### What shipped
- Specific features, specific dates
- 3-5 bullets max
- «Improved performance» = вже weak. Need detail.

### What's next
- 3-4 priorities for next 30 days
- Don't promise unrealistic timelines
- Tie to OKRs if possible

### Things I'm worried about
- 1-3 honest concerns
- NOT «we're a bit behind» (weak)
- «Trial-to-paid is 11% vs 15% target. Hypothesis: onboarding friction. Testing X this month» (strong)

### Going better than expected
- 1-2 wins
- Real numbers
- Don't oversell — investors smell hype

### Asks
- Specific, concrete
- One thing usually best
- Examples:
  - «Intros to 3 YouTubers...»
  - «Thoughts on cost structure of TTS APIs...»
  - «Anyone who's launched в App Store с similar product...»

### Notes
- Optional
- 1-2 sentences max
- Color без being chatty

## Anti-patterns

❌ «We had a great month» — vague, weak
❌ «Crushing it» — Silicon-Valley speak, hates
❌ Mass-blast to 50 people з same template
❌ Skip a bad month — worse than sending
❌ Hide negative data
❌ Bury the ask
❌ Use jargon: «product-market fit» (mean specific things)
❌ Markдown formatting tablets (Outlook breaks)

## What good looks like

**Good update example excerpt:**

> Paid users grew from 1,247 to 1,512 (+21%). MRR $19.3k → $22.8k. But D30 retention dropped from 26% to 21%. Specifically: users on Android who joined post-Aug 15. Hypothesis: missing onboarding fix that we shipped iOS-only. Working on Android fix by Sep 14.

**Why it's good:**
- Numbers specific
- Trend acknowledged honestly
- Hypothesis stated
- Action committed з date

**Bad example:**

> We're seeing strong growth! Users are loving the product. Some engagement metrics need work but we're optimistic.

**Why bad:**
- «Strong» = how strong?
- «Loving» — based on what?
- «Optimistic» — based on what?
- No numbers, no plan, no urgency

---

## How investors actually use these

Most investors:
1. Scan subject line
2. Read metrics top
3. Skim what shipped / what's next
4. Pay attention to «worried about»
5. See ask и either help or not

If you bury the ask, they miss it. If you lie about metrics, you lose them.

## When you're у trouble

If month was bad — extra discipline:

```
Subject: FORM · September 2026 update (M5) · context: tough month

[Be specific about what went wrong]
[Hypothesis для what caused it]
[Concrete action plan]
[Realistic timeline]
[What support would help]
[Calmness, not panic]
```

**Investors prefer hard truth over good news.** Bad update із plan > good update без plan.

## After 12 months

Switch to **quarterly** updates з deeper content. Monthly granularity less useful at scale.

Quarterly format extends template:
- Cohort retention curves
- Business model evolution
- Strategic decisions з context
- Hiring / team updates
- Major risks
- 6-month forward view

---

## Distribution list

Keep small + focused:
- Lead investor(s)
- Other significant investors
- Active advisors
- 3-5 strategic well-wishers

Total: 10-20 people maximum. Don't blast.

Mass-blast = signal noise. Targeted = signal clarity.
