### DEC-080 · OQ-WIN-04: GDPR Art. 17 litigation hold — MSA §11.6 added; DPA Art. 7 processing-basis amendment mechanism

- **Decision:** FORM documents a formal litigation hold procedure in MSA §11.6 addressing OQ-WIN-04 (`docs/COST_MODEL.md §43.11`). When a churned enterprise tenant disputes the final invoice or a legal claim arises, FORM may declare a litigation hold blocking the §12.3 deletion timeline **exclusively for non-health data** (billing records, contract records, SLA performance data, relevant DEC-030 audit log entries). Art. 9 health data is categorically excluded from any litigation hold — the §12.2 zero-grace-period rule remains absolute per DEC-036. Activation requires compliance-officer written instruction countersigned by outside counsel; maximum hold duration 36 months; 5-business-day notice to Customer. Two DEC-030 HMAC-chained events registered: `enterprise.litigation_hold_declared` (HIGH/7yr) and `enterprise.litigation_hold_released` (HIGH/7yr) in `docs/AUDIT_LOG_SCHEMA.md §Enterprise Post-Churn`. DPA Art. 7 processing basis transitions to GDPR Art. 6(1)(f) (legitimate interests) or Art. 6(1)(c) (legal obligation) for retained records — documented as a DPA Art. 7 amendment in MSA §11.6.5. Outside counsel review required before first MSA execution (§11.6.9). Closes OQ-WIN-04 (P1 — before first enterprise MSA signature, M5).
- **Owner:** compliance-officer + outside counsel
- **Why:** (1) **GDPR Art. 17(3)(e) carve-out is real.** GDPR Art. 17(3)(e) explicitly permits retention "for the establishment, exercise or defence of legal claims." Without a documented litigation hold procedure, FORM faces a dilemma: either delete billing records on the standard 90-day schedule (losing evidence needed to defend an ETF dispute under §11.4) or retain records without a documented legal basis (GDPR Art. 5(1)(e) storage-limitation violation). §11.6 resolves this by providing a documented legal basis tied to a specific activation procedure. (2) **Invoice dispute risk is concrete at churn.** §43.11 identified that a churned tenant disputing the final invoice is the most likely trigger — FORM's ETF clause (MSA §11.4) creates financial incentives for such disputes. The 90-day standard deletion timeline in §12.3 is insufficient to reach arbitration closure under §18. (3) **Health data exclusion is non-negotiable.** The DEC-036 zero-grace-period invariant is preserved without exception — §11.6 is explicitly non-applicable to Art. 9 data, closing any ambiguity about whether a litigation hold could ever override the Privacy Floor. (4) **Chain auditability.** Two DEC-030 events provide an HMAC-chained record of hold activation and release, supporting SOC 2 C1.2 and demonstrating that any hold is properly time-bounded, documented, and released.
- **Reverse cost:** Low. §11.6 is an additive clause; removing it reverts FORM to unresolved OQ-WIN-04 status — no documented basis for retaining billing records during invoice disputes. Not recommended before first enterprise MSA signature.

### DEC-079 · OQ-WIN-03: Winback tenant_id policy — new UUID (Option B) adopted; WINBACK-CHAIN-01 amended to event-ID lookup

- **Decision:** When a churned enterprise customer signs a new contract (winback), FORM always provisions a **new `tenant_id` UUID** (Option B). The prior `tenant_id` is never reused. `WinbackConvertedPayload` gains a new field `prior_tenant_id: z.string().uuid()` to make the cross-tenant HMAC chain self-auditable. WINBACK-CHAIN-01 is amended: the `emit-audit-event` Worker validates the chain by looking up the event with `id = winback_initiated_event_id` and verifying it (a) is an `enterprise.winback_initiated` event, (b) was created within 365 days, and (c) has `tenant_id` = `prior_tenant_id` of the converted event — no lookup by "same `tenant_id`". Closes OQ-WIN-03 (P0 — before migration 0085 apply, M10).
- **Owner:** enterprise-architect + compliance-officer
- **Why:** (1) **DEL-E-001 certificate integrity.** `enterprise.deletion_certificate_issued` (CRITICAL/7yr) asserts that all personal data associated with that `tenant_id` UUID has been purged from production systems. Re-activating the same UUID contradicts the legal representation made in the deletion certificate — a GDPR Art. 17 and SOC 2 C1.2 audit-chain integrity breach. Option A is architecturally incompatible with issuing a valid deletion certificate. (2) **HMAC chain self-auditability.** `winback_initiated_event_id` (already in payload) plus new `prior_tenant_id` allows an auditor to reconstruct the chain — `old_tenant → winback_initiated → winback_converted` — solely from the HMAC chain without joining `enterprise_churn_events`. (3) **Event-ID lookup is stricter.** The amended WINBACK-CHAIN-01 check is more tamper-resistant than a `tenant_id`-based query: it pins the predecessor to a specific event UUID, preventing any ambiguity when a customer has multiple churn events on different dates. (4) **Pattern consistency.** The `winback_initiated_event_id` FK pattern mirrors the `deletion_request_event_id` FK in `DeletionCertificateIssuedPayload` — both chain terminal events carry the predecessor event UUID for direct lookup. (5) **No NRR denominator risk.** New `tenant_id` = new contract lifecycle with clean ARR attribution; no risk of a reactivated UUID inflating or distorting NRR formula denominator (§23.1, §43.6.3).
- **Reverse cost:** Medium. Reverting to Option A requires: (a) remove `prior_tenant_id` from `WinbackConvertedPayload`; (b) revert WINBACK-CHAIN-01 Worker to `tenant_id`-based lookup; (c) amend DPA Annex B to disclose that GDPR-deleted `tenant_id` UUIDs may be reactivated — a DPA re-execution with all EU enterprise customers. Not recommended without a specific compelling reason that cannot be addressed by the `prior_tenant_id` cross-reference.

### DEC-066 · OQ-R24-01: Per-tenant SCIM bulk deprovision threshold (default 20%, range 5–100%) with CSM-countersigned time-limited override

- **Decision:** FORM will implement a **per-tenant configurable SCIM bulk deprovision guard** at the SCIM Worker endpoint level (preventive control), complementing the existing reactive `AL-SCIM-MASS-01` pg_cron alert (INCIDENT_RESPONSE.md R-24). The guard rejects any incoming SCIM PUSH that would deprovision ≥ `bdg_threshold_pct`% of a tenant's `contracted_seats` within a **5-minute rolling window**, returning HTTP 422 `BULK_DEPROVISION_THRESHOLD_EXCEEDED`. Closes OQ-R24-01 (P1 — before enterprise GA, M13).
- **Owner:** enterprise-architect + platform-engineer + compliance-officer + customer-success
- **Why:** (1) **Preventive vs. reactive gap.** The existing AL-SCIM-MASS-01 alert (INCIDENT_RESPONSE.md R-24) is reactive — it detects a mass deprovisioning event after it has already occurred (pg_cron every 5 min). The mean detection-to-response window is 5–10 minutes, during which affected employees lose access. The bulk deprovision guard fires at the SCIM endpoint level before any deprovisioning write reaches Postgres — eliminating the detection-to-response gap for accidental bulk pushes entirely. (2) **H3 root cause prevention.** R-24's root cause hypothesis H3 (FORM-side SCIM processing bug) is specifically closed by a threshold guard: if a FORM bug causes SCIM to process a mass-delete payload, the guard blocks it at the Worker level before any `UPDATE form_users SET status = 'deprovisioned'` executes. H1 (IdP group deletion) and H2 (SCIM filter bug) are similarly mitigated — the guard is IdP-agnostic. (3) **Override path is necessary.** Rejecting all decision without an override path would break legitimate layoff workflows. The CSM-countersigned override with PAM audit trail provides the required exception mechanism while creating a compliance-grade record (two DEC-030 events: `scim.bulk_deprovision_override_issued` + `scim.bulk_deprovision_override_used`). Auto-revocation after first use ensures the override cannot be re-used for a second bulk push without a new CSM countersign. (4) **Per-tenant threshold is pragmatic.** A global threshold would break tenants with legitimate large-batch workflows. Configurable range 5–100% lets each tenant calibrate the guard to their operational reality. CSM countersign for extreme values (≤ 10% or ≥ 50%) adds a check on tenants who would effectively disable the guard. (5) **Five-minute rolling window prevents latency gaming.** A single-request threshold could be circumvented by spreading deprovisioning across multiple small SCIM pushes. The `scim:deprov_guard:{tenantId}:{windowKey}` KV counter accumulates deprovisioned-seat counts across all SCIM requests within the same 5-minute epoch bucket — successive pushes within the window trigger the guard even if each individual push is below threshold.
- **Reverse cost:** Medium. (4) **SIEM-CONSENT-01 technology enforcement.** Making the chain invariant block emission — not just display a warning — converts this from a procedural control to a technology control. - **Owner:** devops-lead + security-engineer + compliance-officer
- **Why:** (1) **Threshold confirmed correct.** Event volume baseline analysis (§44.2.1) shows that the 30-min zero-event probability during business hours drops to LOW at pre-beta (≥ 1 active user) and to NEGLIGIBLE at consumer-beta scale. A 60-min threshold (Option (b)) would increase the undetected-stall window from 30 min to 60 min — during which up to 60 min of HMAC chain events could be silently lost. (2) **Business-hours gate already resolves the off-peak concern.** OQ-ANOM-02 raised concern about 02:00–06:00 UTC off-peak false positives. §27.7 already gates AL-SIEM-06 to 08:00–22:00 UTC — the off-peak concern is fully resolved by the existing specification, not a new mechanism. Reverting to immediate P1 activation (removing the shadow mode) requires only updating the Better Stack alert routing on day 1 of deployment and removing the `system.siem_alert_activated` shadow-mode emission step. Increasing the threshold to 60 min requires updating §27.7, a new DECISION_LOG entry (DEC-056-REV), and evidence that the annual false-positive review (§44.6 item 10) triggered the change. ### DEC-040 · Feature flag lifecycle: plan downgrade auto-disables Growth+ flags (OQ-FLAG-01); 90-day deprecation sunset policy (OQ-FLAG-02)

- **Decision:** Two decisions for the feature flag lifecycle in `docs/DATA_MODEL.md §19`:
  - **OQ-FLAG-01 (High — before M5):** When a tenant downgrades from Growth to Starter (or from Enterprise to Growth/Starter), **Option (a) is adopted:** all flags whose `feature_flag_registry.minimum_tier IN ('growth', 'enterprise')` are automatically disabled in `tenant_feature_flags` via a plan-downgrade hook wired into the contract lifecycle (`DATA_MODEL.md §16 tenant.lifecycle_status`). Each disabled flag emits a `feature_flag.disabled` DEC-030 HMAC-chained event with `reason = 'plan_downgrade'` and `set_by = 'system'`. Rows are set to `enabled = FALSE` (not deleted) so the disable is explicitly re-enabled on re-upgrade — no silent reactivation. The downgrade hook fires synchronously inside the same transaction as the `tenants.plan` column update and uses `FOR UPDATE SKIP LOCKED` to avoid TOCTOU races with concurrent feature-flag reads.
  - **OQ-FLAG-02 (Medium — before first deprecation):** The flag deprecation sunset process is formalised as: (a) minimum **90 days** between setting `deprecated_at` on a `feature_flag_registry` row and the effective disablement date; (b) customer-success communicates via a non-dismissible admin dashboard banner when a tenant has an enabled flag whose `deprecated_at IS NOT NULL` (banner text: *"Feature [flag_label] will be retired on [deprecated_at + 90d]. Contact your CSM to plan the transition."*); (c) a pg_cron job runs nightly at 03:15 UTC and soft-deletes `tenant_feature_flags` rows where the corresponding registry `deprecated_at + INTERVAL '90 days' <= now()`, emitting `feature_flag.disabled` DEC-030 events with `reason = 'deprecated'` for each row. Hard-delete is explicitly rejected — rows remain as tombstones to preserve the historical audit trail.
- **Owner:** enterprise-architect + compliance-officer
- **Why:**
  - *OQ-FLAG-01:* Without the downgrade hook, a Starter tenant retains `enabled = TRUE` in `tenant_feature_flags` for Growth-only flags after a plan downgrade. `assertFeatureEnabled()` blocks at the Worker tier gate, so there is no functional access regression — but the inconsistent stored rows mislead `form_admin` operators, pollute the audit trail, and would silently re-activate Growth features on any future re-upgrade without a deliberate admin action. This violates SOC 2 CC8.1 (change management requires explicit, logged authorisation for feature enablement). Option (a) produces clean data and a correct audit chain at negligible implementation cost.
  - *OQ-FLAG-02:* Enterprise customers depend on flagged features for production integrations (e.g. `audit.siem_export_enabled`, `admin.cohort_analytics_enabled`). Surprise removal without notice causes customer outages and erodes trust. A 90-day documented sunset policy satisfies SOC 2 CC9.2 (vendor and partner obligations) and creates a defensible change management record. Soft-delete preserves evidence of what features each tenant held and for how long — necessary for SOC 2 audits and GDPR Art. 30 records of processing.
- **Reverse cost:**
  - *OQ-FLAG-01:* Medium. Switching to Option (b) — rely solely on the Worker tier gate — requires: removing the downgrade hook, updating the SOC 2 CC8.1 narrative (losing the "explicit re-enable" guarantee), and notifying any tenant already downgraded under this rule if their flag rows were set to `enabled = FALSE`. Permissible before the first downgrade event is processed; increases in cost with each downgrade processed under this rule.
  - *OQ-FLAG-02:* Low before any 90-day sunset notice is dispatched. Once a sunset banner is shown to a tenant, the date is a commitment — cannot be shortened without a retroactive notification and amended CSM communication.

## Format guidelines

- **One decision per row.** Never bundle.
- **Owner mandatory.** Who can be cited if questioned.
- **"Why" in plain language.** Not «strategic alignment»; the actual reason.
- **Reverse cost honest.** «Low» / «Medium» / «High» — what does undoing cost?

### Don't log:

- Daily micro-decisions (what color text to use)
- Personal preferences без impact
- Decisions still у flux (log when final)

### Do log:

- Anything affecting roadmap
- Anything affecting safety or ethics
- Anything affecting hiring or org
- Anything that future-you might question
