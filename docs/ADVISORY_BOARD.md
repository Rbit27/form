# FORM · Advisory Board Playbook

> Власник: founder  
> Статус: pre-product · target close before Beta Q3 2026  
> Мета: 3–4 signed advisors that make the product safer, more credible, and harder to copy

---

## TL;DR

We need advisors who will push back, not just sign. Four profiles: sport science, clinical/psychology, computer vision, and industry. Compensation is modest equity (0.1–0.25% per slot) + time commitment of 6–10 hours/month. Priority: sport science + clinical advisors before any user-facing claims ship.

---

## 1 · Why an advisory board (and why now)

We have 14 AI agents and a rigorous internal review process. That's not the same as expert humans who will tell us we're wrong.

Three specific risks an advisory board mitigates:

**Science drift.** An AI sports scientist trained on the literature is good. But it doesn't have a conversation with a PhD who just finished a trial on HRV interpretation in untrained vs. trained populations. Human sport science advisors catch our blind spots.

**Clinical liability.** Our product touches training load, food logging, and body-related feedback. That's close enough to clinical territory that we need someone with a clinical psychology or eating disorder research background to review our defaults before we ship.

**Credibility signals.** A product built by one person with AI agents can ship — but raising a seed round and recruiting serious engineers goes faster when there are two or three named advisors who carry domain credibility. This is a practical reality of early-stage fundraising in health/wellness.

## 2 · The four advisor profiles

### Slot 1 · Sport Science (priority: HIGH — before Beta)

**What we need:** Someone who can validate our training methodology, programming logic, and HRV-integration approach. Someone who will tell us when we're overstating what the science supports.

**Profile:**
- Practical background: sports science, exercise physiology, strength and conditioning
- Familiar with HRV, periodization theory, and progressive overload evidence base
- Has worked with real athletes or coaches, not just academic settings
- Can read training programs and identify errors in recovery logic
- Not required: academic affiliation (industry practitioner is fine, often better)

**Anti-profile:**
- Pure academic with no applied experience
- Fitness-industry influencer with "advisor" in their bio for 15 different companies
- No published work or equivalent practitioner track record

**How to find:**
- Conference networks: NSCA, ACSM, BASES (UK), ASCA (Australia)
- LinkedIn: search "strength and conditioning" + "research" + university affiliation
- Podcast guests on MASS Research Review, Stronger By Science, Iron Culture
- S&C coaches at professional sports clubs open to part-time advisory work
- Twitter/X: follow evidence-based S&C community, identify practitioners with public intellectual output

**What we ask:**
- 8 hours/month: one program review, one 60-min call, copy review, one push-back session, async Q&A
- Quarterly: review our HRV interpretation logic, validate training research citations

**Why they'd say yes:**
- Meaningful equity in a consumer product (most advisory roles in sports tech are token-level)
- Genuine influence on product design — not "we ran it by our advisor" theater
- Early access to beta user data that's interesting from a research perspective (with user consent)
- Visibility if the product succeeds

---

### Slot 2 · Clinical / Psychology (priority: HIGH — before any food or body feature ships)

**What we need:** Someone with a clinical psychology or eating disorder research background who can review our defaults, our Victor voice on body-related topics, and our food logging design. This person has VETO-adjacent authority alongside our `clinical-safety` AI agent.

**Profile:**
- Background in clinical psychology, eating disorder treatment, or health psychology
- Familiar with how fitness apps can inadvertently harm users with sub-clinical ED risk
- Can review copy and UX flows for harm potential — not just obvious triggers but subtle ones
- Willing to engage with a pre-product company to shape defaults before launch (not just review post-hoc)

**Anti-profile:**
- Academic-only with no exposure to consumer digital health products
- Someone who sees every fitness app as inherently harmful (useful perspective, wrong advisor profile)
- Motivational coach who uses clinical-sounding language without clinical training

**How to find:**
- Academic networks: psychology departments with health or eating disorder research tracks
- Clinical practice: dietitians or clinical psychologists who also engage publicly on fitness culture
- Organizations: National Eating Disorders Association (NEDA), eating disorder charities in UK/Europe
- Conference networks: AEDFA, IICSA, European Eating Disorders Association
- LinkedIn: search "eating disorder" + "digital health" or "fitness technology"

**What we ask:**
- 6 hours/month: review one product output per month, one 45-min call, copy review for any food/body-adjacent content
- Hard requirement: review of food logging feature design, Victor voice guidelines for body topics, and ED-screening flow before beta opens

**Why they'd say yes:**
- Real influence before bad defaults ship, not post-launch damage control
- Unique position: shaping how a consumer fitness product handles clinical risk at the design level — rare opportunity
- Academic credential opportunity: "consulting on clinical safety design for AI fitness" is publishable interest
- Equity if product reaches scale

---

### Slot 3 · Computer Vision / ML (priority: MEDIUM — before CV feature ships)

**What we need:** An ML engineer or researcher with practical experience in human pose estimation, action recognition, or on-device inference. Someone who can tell us when our CV accuracy claims are credible and when they're not.

**Profile:**
- Hands-on experience with MediaPipe, Apple Vision, or equivalent pose estimation frameworks
- Has built or shipped on-device ML models (not just trained in the cloud)
- Understands inference latency and battery constraints on mobile
- Can review our CV pipeline architecture and tell us where the failure modes are
- Not required: academic background in CV (practitioner with shipped products is equally valuable)

**Anti-profile:**
- Research scientist with no production ML experience
- Someone who builds cloud-only CV pipelines
- Anyone whose primary reference is "we'll fine-tune a foundation model and it'll be fine"

**How to find:**
- GitHub: contributors to MediaPipe, OpenPose, MMPose
- Conference networks: CVPR, ICCV, ECCV (practitioner tracks)
- LinkedIn: "computer vision" + "iOS" + "on-device" or "CoreML"
- Twitter/X: ML engineers who share applied inference work
- Communities: r/MachineLearning, ML Discord servers

**What we ask:**
- 6 hours/month: one architecture review, async Q&A on CV questions, review of any public CV accuracy claims before they're published
- Before Beta: review of our CV pipeline design and honest assessment of likely accuracy vs. our claims

**Why they'd say yes:**
- Interesting product — consumer-facing pose estimation with real user feedback loop
- Equity at pre-seed valuation
- Option to co-author on any technical writeup about our CV approach (with consent)

---

### Slot 4 · Fitness Industry (priority: LOW — post-seed)

**What we need:** Someone with distribution or business model experience in the consumer fitness industry. A former founder, executive, or operator from a comparable company. Not a celebrity — an operator who knows how fitness apps actually acquire, retain, and monetize users.

**Profile:**
- Operated or led a consumer fitness product at 100k+ users
- Has seen at least one full product cycle: launch → scale → plateau → pivot
- Understands App Store dynamics for health and fitness
- Has relevant network: potential partners, press, early hires in the fitness space
- Not required: current operator (ex-founder/exec is fine)

**When to engage:** After seed close. This profile is about distribution leverage, which matters more once we have a product to distribute.

## 3 · What we offer

### Equity

Standard advisor equity for pre-seed consumer app:

| Profile | Equity | Vesting |
|---|---|---|
| Sport Science (Slot 1) | 0.15–0.25% | 2 years, monthly, no cliff |
| Clinical / Psychology (Slot 2) | 0.15–0.20% | 2 years, monthly, no cliff |
| CV / ML (Slot 3) | 0.20–0.25% | 2 years, monthly, no cliff |
| Industry (Slot 4) | 0.10–0.15% | 2 years, monthly, no cliff |

No cliff because we need engagement immediately. Monthly vesting because we want to keep the relationship active.

**Option pool impact:** Total advisory board = ~0.65–0.85% of option pool. At current stage, this is appropriate. After Seed close, replenish option pool.

### Cash retainer

**Pre-Seed (now):** No cash retainer. Equity only. If an advisor requires cash upfront, that's a sign they're not bought-in enough for us at this stage.

**Post-Seed:** Consider $500–1,000/month retainer for Slots 1 and 2 if they're providing significant time. This is a retention mechanism, not compensation for market-rate consulting.

### Access

- Direct line to founder — not filtered through team
- Early access to beta (once it exists)
- Named credit on site and press materials (unless they prefer anonymous)
- Input on product decisions before they ship, not after
- Ability to exit the advisory role with 30 days notice (no long lock-ins)

## 4 · How to approach advisors

### Warm intro (preferred path)

Priority order for intros:
1. Other advisors (once Slot 1 is filled, use them to intro Slot 2)
2. Investors (even before a formal raise — angels and scout funds know researchers)
3. Academic network (university sports science departments, former colleagues)
4. Conference connections

Template for a warm intro request:

```
Subject: Would you be willing to make an intro?

Hi [Introducer],

I'm building FORM — an AI personal trainer that uses computer vision
for form feedback and HRV for program adaptation. Pre-product, serious
about the science, one founder right now.

I'm looking for a sport science advisor who would be willing to push
back on our training methodology and HRV logic. I think [Name] would be
exactly the right person — the work they did on [specific relevant thing]
is directly relevant to what we're building.

Would you be willing to make a warm intro? I'd keep it brief — a 20-min
call to see if there's mutual interest.

GitHub: https://github.com/Rbit27/form
George
```

### Cold outreach (fallback)

Use only if no warm path exists. Cold outreach to academics works better than cold outreach to industry practitioners.

Template:

```
Subject: Sport science advisor role — AI training app (pre-product, 0.20% equity)

Hi [Name],

I'm George, building FORM — an AI personal trainer that adapts programming
daily based on HRV and sleep, with camera-based form feedback on 8 base lifts.

I've read your work on [specific paper/post — be specific]. The [specific finding]
directly informs how we're thinking about [specific design decision].

I'm looking for a sport science advisor: 8 hours/month, meaningful equity
(0.20%), real influence on product decisions before they ship. Not a
letterhead advisor — someone who will tell us when we're wrong.

The full scope is in our public repo:
https://github.com/Rbit27/form/hiring/jd-sport-science-advisor.md

Would you be open to a 20-minute call to see if there's interest?

George Rbit27
Founder, FORM · Kyiv
george@form.coach
```

Rules for cold outreach:
- **One specific reference to their work** — if you can't name one specific thing they've done that's relevant, don't send the email
- **No flattery paragraph** — just the reference and why it's relevant
- **Full transparency about stage** — "pre-product" not "stealth startup"
- **Ask for 20 minutes**, not an advisor relationship

### The 20-minute call

Goal: mutual interest check, not pitch.

Order:
1. What we're building (2 min)
2. Why we need this advisor profile specifically (2 min)
3. Ask them about their current work and interests (8 min)
4. Explain the advisor scope — time, equity, influence (3 min)
5. Ask: does this seem like a fit? (2 min)
6. If yes: agree on a 30-day trial with one concrete deliverable (3 min)

**Red flags during the call:**
- They talk more about their other advisory roles than their substantive work
- They ask about publicity before asking about the product
- They agree with everything you say (you want pushback, not yes-people)
- They have no questions about technical accuracy of your claims

## 5 · Trial period

Before signing an advisory agreement, run a 30-day trial:

1. Give them one concrete deliverable: "Review this training program template and tell us what's wrong"
2. One 60-min call
3. Evaluate: do they actually push back? Are they engaged? Does their feedback improve the work?

If yes → proceed to agreement.  
If no → thank them, don't sign.

**Most advisory relationships fail because they never had a trial period.** Don't skip this.

---

## 6 · Advisory agreement

Use a standard advisor agreement (FAST Agreement from Founder Institute is industry-standard and free):

Key terms to negotiate:
- **Equity:** As above per profile
- **Vesting:** 2 years monthly, no cliff — start from signing date
- **Time commitment:** Defined in hours/month, not vague
- **Intellectual property:** Advisors don't own anything they contribute — clarify upfront
- **Confidentiality:** Standard NDA language
- **Exit:** 30-day written notice by either party, equity stops vesting
- **Non-compete:** Do NOT ask for one. Advisors work with multiple companies. Asking for non-compete will lose you the advisor.

**Get legal review before signing.** Use the same counsel reviewing our privacy policy and ToS.

## 8 · What good looks like (1 year from now)

- Sport science advisor has reviewed every major training methodology decision before it shipped
- Clinical advisor has signed off on every food/body-adjacent feature and copy

- Neither advisor has been just a name on a website
- Both have pushed back on at least three things we originally intended to ship

That's the bar.
