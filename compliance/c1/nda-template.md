## FORM NON-DISCLOSURE AND CONFIDENTIALITY AGREEMENT

**This Non-Disclosure and Confidentiality Agreement** ("Agreement") is entered into as of **[EFFECTIVE DATE]** ("Effective Date") by and between:

**FORM** (full legal entity name: **[LEGAL ENTITY NAME TO BE INSERTED AT INCORPORATION]**, a company incorporated under the laws of **[JURISDICTION]**, with its registered address at **[REGISTERED ADDRESS]**) ("Company"); and

**[FULL LEGAL NAME OF INDIVIDUAL]**, residing at **[HOME ADDRESS]**, holder of identification document **[DOCUMENT TYPE AND NUMBER]** ("Recipient").

Company and Recipient are each referred to herein as a "Party" and together as the "Parties."

**WHEREAS**, the Company wishes to engage Recipient in the capacity of **[JOB TITLE / ROLE]** (the "Role"); and

**WHEREAS**, in connection with the Role, Recipient will receive access to Confidential Information (as defined below), including special-category personal health data belonging to FORM users and enterprise tenants as defined by GDPR Article 9; and

**WHEREAS**, the Parties wish to establish the terms governing Recipient's obligations with respect to such Confidential Information;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein and for good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows.

---

## 1. Definitions

**1.1 "Confidential Information"** means all non-public information, in any form or medium, that the Company discloses or makes available to Recipient, or to which Recipient gains access in connection with the Role, including without limitation:

(a) **Technical information** — source code, model weights, training datasets, CV pose estimation pipeline, proprietary algorithms, system architecture, API specifications, infrastructure configurations, security credentials, and cryptographic material;

(b) **User and health data** — all personal data processed by FORM on behalf of its users and enterprise tenants, and specifically all data categories constituting special-category data under GDPR Article 9(1), including but not limited to:
- Workout and exercise session records (sets, reps, load, duration);
- Computer vision pose and form feedback data;
- Wearable sensor readings (heart rate, HRV, sleep, resting metrics);
- Meal logs, macronutrient records, and body composition data;
- AI coaching session transcripts ("coaching turns");
- Biometric inference data generated by FORM's AI systems;
- Any mental health or recovery-state signals derived from the above;

(c) **Business and financial information** — pricing models, enterprise customer identities and contract terms, investor information, financial projections, roadmap, OKRs, partnership discussions, and acquisition-related information;

(d) **Operational information** — incident reports, security findings, penetration test results, vulnerability disclosures, and internal compliance documentation;

(e) **Third-party information** — information belonging to FORM's enterprise tenants, sub-processors, partners, or advisors that FORM holds under a duty of confidentiality.

**1.2** Confidential Information does not require marking as "confidential" to be protected under this Agreement. The health data categories enumerated in Section 1.1(b) are Confidential Information regardless of their labeling in any system.

---

## 2. Confidentiality Obligations

**2.1 Strict confidence.** Recipient shall hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not, directly or indirectly, disclose, publish, transmit, sell, or otherwise make available any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of an authorized representative of the Company.

**2.2 Use limitation.** Recipient shall use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing the duties of the Role. No use for personal benefit, secondary employment, competitive analysis, academic publication, or any purpose outside the Role is permitted.

**2.3 Standard of care.** Recipient shall protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care used to protect Recipient's own confidential information, and in no event less than reasonable care as required by GDPR Article 32 for personal data processing.

**2.4 Need-to-know.** Recipient shall not disclose Confidential Information to any colleague, subcontractor, or team member unless that person (a) has a legitimate need to know in connection with the Role and (b) is themselves bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations.

**2.5 Prompt notification.** Recipient shall promptly notify Company at **security@form.coach** — within **one (1) hour** of discovery — of any suspected or actual unauthorized disclosure, loss, theft, or breach involving Confidential Information. This one-hour internal reporting obligation feeds the Company's 72-hour GDPR Article 33 supervisory authority notification clock and must be treated as a hard deadline.

---

## 3. Exclusions from Confidential Information

The obligations in Section 2 do not apply to information that Recipient can demonstrate by contemporaneous written records:

(a) was, at the time of disclosure, already publicly known through no act or omission of Recipient;

(b) becomes publicly known after disclosure through no act or omission of Recipient;

(c) was rightfully known by Recipient prior to disclosure without any obligation of confidentiality;

(d) is received from a third party who is not subject to any obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information; or

(e) is required to be disclosed by applicable law, court order, or binding governmental authority — provided that Recipient gives Company the maximum practicable prior written notice to enable Company to seek a protective order, and that Recipient discloses only the minimum information legally required.

**Health data exception:** The exclusions in (a) through (d) do not apply to personal health data described in Section 1.1(b). Such data retains its character as Confidential Information and as special-category data under GDPR Article 9 regardless of public availability of the individual data subject's identity or health status from other sources.

---

## 4. Data Protection Obligations

**4.1 Processor status.** Recipient acknowledges that in the course of the Role, Recipient may act as an authorised processor of personal data (including special-category health data) on behalf of the Company and its enterprise tenants. Recipient shall at all times comply with:

(a) GDPR Articles 5, 9, 28, 29, 32, and 33 as applicable to the processing activities of the Role;

(b) `docs/SECURITY.md` (FORM's security policy — available on the internal repository), including the break-glass access procedure for any elevated access to production systems;

(c) FORM's Data Processing Guidelines and Access Control Policy as communicated during onboarding and as updated from time to time.

**4.2 Processing only as directed.** Recipient shall process personal data only on documented instructions from the Company. Recipient shall immediately notify the Company if Recipient believes any instruction infringes GDPR or applicable data protection law.

**4.3 No secondary processing.** Recipient shall not use, copy, analyze, export, or transmit any personal data processed in connection with the Role for any purpose other than performance of the Role, including for training external machine learning models, academic research, or personal analytics.

**4.4 Deletion on termination.** Upon termination of this Agreement or the Role, Recipient shall immediately delete or return all copies of personal data in Recipient's possession, and shall certify deletion in writing within three (3) calendar days of the termination date.

**4.5 Audit cooperation.** Recipient shall cooperate with any reasonable audit or inspection by the Company or its designated compliance officer relating to Recipient's compliance with this Section 4.

---

## 5. Intellectual Property Assignment

**5.1 Work for hire.** All work product, inventions, discoveries, improvements, processes, formulas, algorithms, code, model weights, training data annotations, computer vision pipeline enhancements, coaching prompt templates, and any other intellectual property created by Recipient during the term of the Role and in connection with the Role (collectively, "Work Product") shall be considered "work made for hire" under applicable law and shall be the exclusive property of the Company.

**5.2 Assignment.** To the extent any Work Product is not considered "work made for hire" under applicable law, Recipient hereby irrevocably assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to such Work Product, including all intellectual property rights therein, without further consideration.

**5.3 ML artefacts.** The assignment in Sections 5.1 and 5.2 explicitly covers all machine learning artefacts created in connection with the Role, including model checkpoints, fine-tuned weights, labelled datasets, evaluation scripts, and LoRA adapters, regardless of whether such artefacts were created on Company hardware or Recipient's personal equipment.

**5.4 Moral rights waiver.** To the fullest extent permitted by applicable law, Recipient waives all moral rights (including rights of attribution and integrity) in and to the Work Product.

**5.5 Assistance.** Recipient shall promptly execute any documents and take any actions reasonably requested by the Company to perfect, record, or enforce the Company's ownership of Work Product in any jurisdiction.

---

## 6. Non-Solicitation

**6.1 Non-solicitation of employees.** During the term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months following its termination for any reason, Recipient shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, or induce any employee or contractor of the Company to leave the Company's employ or engagement.

**6.2 Non-solicitation of customers.** During the term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months following its termination for any reason, Recipient shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit or divert any enterprise customer of the Company with whom Recipient had material contact during the Role for the purpose of providing services competitive with those of the Company.

**6.3 Counsel review note.** The enforceability of non-solicitation covenants varies by jurisdiction (Ukraine, EU member states). Outside counsel must confirm the scope and duration of these restrictions is enforceable in the Recipient's jurisdiction of residence before this Agreement is executed.

---

## 7. Term and Survival

**7.1 Term.** This Agreement is effective from the Effective Date and remains in effect for the duration of the Role and for five (5) years following termination of the Role for any reason ("Confidentiality Period").

**7.2 Health data survival.** Notwithstanding Section 7.1, obligations under Section 2 and Section 4 relating to personal health data described in Section 1.1(b) survive termination of this Agreement **indefinitely**. The Company's obligations under GDPR to protect the data subjects whose health data Recipient processed do not expire; neither do Recipient's corresponding duties.

**7.3 IP survival.** The assignments in Section 5 and the obligations in Sections 3–4 of Section 5 survive termination of this Agreement indefinitely.

---

## 8. Remedies

**8.1 Irreparable harm.** Recipient acknowledges that any breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable injury to the Company for which monetary damages would be inadequate, and that the Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, and any other equitable remedies available without posting bond or other security, in addition to all other remedies available at law or equity.

**8.2 GDPR penalties.** Recipient acknowledges that unauthorized disclosure of special-category health data constituting a GDPR Article 9 breach may expose both the Company and, in certain circumstances, Recipient personally to regulatory fines under GDPR Article 83 of up to €20,000,000 or 4% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher.

**8.3 Indemnification.** Recipient shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Company from any claims, damages, fines, penalties, legal fees, and costs arising from Recipient's breach of this Agreement.

---

## 9. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

**9.1 Governing law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of **Ukraine** (without giving effect to any choice of law or conflict of law rules that would require application of another jurisdiction's laws), **provided that** all obligations relating to the processing of personal data shall be governed by EU GDPR to the extent that GDPR applies to such processing, and EU GDPR shall take precedence over Ukrainian law in the event of any conflict with respect to data protection obligations.

**9.2 Jurisdiction.** The Parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the **Kyiv Commercial Court** (or, for disputes involving an individual Recipient who is not a business entity, the Kyiv District Court at the Company's registered address) for resolution of any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement. 

**9.3 Amendment note.** Governing law and jurisdiction shall be reviewed and updated at Company incorporation. If the primary operating entity is incorporated in a different jurisdiction (e.g., Estonia, Cyprus, Delaware), this clause shall be amended accordingly prior to first use.

## 10. General Provisions

**10.1 Entire agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior discussions, representations, or agreements relating to confidentiality obligations.

**10.2 Amendment.** No amendment to this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by both Parties. Template amendments require compliance-officer review and an updated entry in `compliance/policy-approval-log.csv`.

**10.3 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, such provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force.

**10.4 Waiver.** The failure of either Party to enforce any right under this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of that right.

**10.5 No employment or engagement created.** This Agreement does not, by itself, create or evidence an employment relationship or engagement. Employment terms are governed by a separate offer letter or services agreement.

**10.6 Counterparts / Electronic signatures.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be an original. Execution by DocuSign qualified electronic signature (eIDAS Regulation Article 26/25) or equivalent shall be binding.
