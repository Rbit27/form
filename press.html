§ 03 · Founder bio

George.
Builder.
Lifter.

Short bio · 50 words

George (Rbit27) is the founder of FORM. With ten years in software engineering and a personal training practice, he started FORM to close the gap between sports-science research and the products available to self-coached lifters.

Medium bio · 100 words

George (Rbit27) is the founder of FORM, an AI personal trainer launched from Kyiv in 2026. Prior to FORM, he spent a decade as a software engineer in product companies. His personal training practice — six years of self-coached lifting, two cycles of working with human trainers, and stints with most fitness apps in the market — convinced him that the gap between modern sports science and consumer products was structural and product-shaped. FORM is his attempt to close it.

Extended bio · for interview context

George started programming at 14, took his first software-engineering role at 19, and has shipped consumer products for the past decade. He started lifting seriously at 22 — first with a personal trainer, then self-coached, eventually realising that no commercial fitness product gave him what an expert coach gave: someone who watched his technique and adjusted his program based on what they saw that day.

His professional background: 10 years of software engineering with consumer-facing product companies. He has personally tried Whoop, Oura, Fitbod, MacroFactor, MyFitnessPal, and Hevy — and has used a human personal trainer for two distinct cycles.

His insight: the gap between modern sports science (HRV-driven programming, RPE autoregulation, evidence-based periodization) and the products available to serious self-coached lifters is structural. None of the major incumbents are built coaching-first.

FORM is his attempt to ship a coaching-first product that is honest about what AI can and cannot do, respects the user’s time and body, and refuses to use the manipulative patterns common in the fitness industry.

He trains four days a week, reads non-fiction, and lives in Kyiv.

§ 06 · Story angles

Five
angles.
Your call.

Looking for an entry point? These are the angles we think are genuinely interesting. We will cooperate on all of them, including critical ones.

01

“AI trainer from Ukraine”

Founder story + wartime context + a product that targets a Western market from Kyiv. The honest version of a startup origin story: no easy VC money, no San Francisco comfort zone, a forcing function that makes you ship something actually good.

02

“1 founder + 14 AI agents = company”

A new operating model for early-stage consumer SaaS. Not AGI hype — a structured team where each agent has one mandate and one veto right. A clinical-safety agent blocks anything that could harm users. What happens when you design an organization this way?

03

“Why all AI fitness apps are still bad”

Critical angle: we’ve done an honest teardown of 8 competitors. None solve the core coaching problem. The fitness industry has an honesty problem, and AI is making it worse, not better. FORM’s answer: enforced discipline at the product level.

04

“Can a phone camera replace a trainer?”

Technology story with honest limitation disclosure. What Apple Vision actually does at 30fps. What “form analysis” can and cannot mean on a consumer device. The difference between gross-deviation detection and professional biomechanical analysis.

05

“Fitness without surveillance”

Consumer privacy angle. The data model that doesn’t sell your workouts to insurance companies. The AI coach that doesn’t comment on your food in real-time. The streak that doesn’t shame you for one missed day.

§ 07 · Journalist FAQ

Straight
answers.

Q: How many users do you have?

Zero paying users. We are pre-product. No app exists yet — mobile development begins with the Founding Engineer hire. Closed beta is planned for ~200 lifters on TestFlight, August 2026. We do not claim traction we do not have.

Q: Why 14 AI agents instead of one model?

Each agent has one mandate and one veto right. clinical-safety stops anything that could harm users. brand-voice maintains consistency. product-strategist kills feature creep. This isn’t marketing — it’s organizational design that fixes the typical AI-startup problem of “one prompt does everything.”

Q: Who are your competitors?

Whoop (data, no program), Fitbod (program, no coaching), Future ($149/mo with human coaches), MacroFactor (nutrition only). None close the same gap: camera + voice + recovery + nutrition + program in one product.

Q: Is this safe to use?

Safety is the first question, not the last. A clinical-safety agent has HARD VETO on anything touching food, body image, mental health, or harsh tone. ED screening on onboarding is non-skippable. Pain triage always routes to a doctor. Safety standards follow NEDA and APA guidelines.

Q: Are you raising money?

Seed round, target $1.5–2.5M, planned close Q3 2026. Investor relations: invest@form.coach.

Q: Why Ukraine?

That’s where the founder is, and that’s where the team will be built. Ukrainian tech works with discipline and lower cost structure. The war is a context, not an excuse. The first market is Ukraine; Western EU is phase 2.
